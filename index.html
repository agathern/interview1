Raquel Trevino

Where are you from?

My parents are from the North of Mexico, but I was born in San Antonio, Texas. I mostly feel Mexican as my parents prioritized my siblings and me to remember our roots. Therefore I am very familiar with Mexican culture, and it is what I identify to the most.

Where did you grow up?

I moved from Texas to Geneva, Switzerland, at the age of 4, and I have been here ever since.

How was it growing up there?

I feel incredibly privileged to have grown up in Geneva. I do not think I had the same experiences as most people I have met in university so far. It is exceptionally safe, so I have always had a lot of freedom. I also went to an international school, so

diversity has always felt familiar to me.

My friends ranged from Greek, English, Australian, Russian, Israeli, and many other cultures.

How did you handle quarantine?

Here in Switzerland, we never went into full lockdown as the hospitals weren not necessarily overflowing. We also did not feel the need to fully lockdown since most people respect each other enough to follow the rules without being asked. So luckily, my experience wasn not too bad. We could still go out to fields with a maximum of five people.

What are your hobbies?

My biggest hobby is probably photography. I particularly enjoy editing the images I take. I could spend hours editing the picture of coffee.

Is there anything in particular that attracted you to photography?

I got my first camera for Christmas when I was twelve. I just always enjoyed taking pictures. At first, I would mostly take it on trips; however I quickly moved on from travel photography as I realised I did not necessarily enjoy taking landscape photos. What interested me more were portraits as

I like capturing human expressions
